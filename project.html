Plan to travel anywhere during the summer?

Said no one in 2020.

Okay I'm pretty sure cancelling a trip to Japan is the least of worries when the COVID-19 virus began to spread around the world. Many dealt with problems such as shutting down businesses, isolated from family/friends, and loss of loved ones. However, that trip cancellation like many others meant a huge decline in business for the people in the tourist industry which makes up 10% of the global gdp, worth around 9 trillion dollars. Let's find out how much of a toll COVID had on tourism around the world, which countries' tourism were effected the most, and by how much?

Quarantine = No Business

Starting in the Hubei Province of China, majority of all the countries in the world began implementing preventive measures in late March / early April of 2020. For most countries the severity of the measures depended on the control and containment of COVID-19 infected individuals. Besides lockdowns and closing of businesses, countries also prioritzed the control on international travel.

The choropleth only shows data for the top ten countries based on their incoming international arrivals. Days with bans on high-risk regions, and full border closure are also included.

Who's the most impacted?

Besides a potentially fatal virus, quaranting upon arrival was a huge factor in the decline of international travel. At the peak of COVID infections and deaths and even up to the end of 2021, quarantining was around 5 to 14 days long, and travelers weren't willing to spend a majority or their entirety of their vacation time in a hotel room.
