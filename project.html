Plan to travel anywhere during the summer?

Said no one in 2020.

Okay I'm pretty sure cancelling a trip to Paris is the least of worries when the COVID-19 virus began to spread around the world. Many dealt with problems such as shutting down businesses, isolated from family/friends, and loss of loved ones. However, that trip cancellation like many others meant a huge decline in business for the people in the tourist industry which makes up 10% of the global gdp, worth around 9 trillion dollars. Let's find out how much of a toll COVID had on tourism around the world, which countries' tourism were effected the most, and by how much?

Quarantine = No Business

Starting in the Hubei Province of China, majority of all the countries in the world began implementing preventive measures in late March / early April of 2020. For most countries the severity of the measures depended on the control and containment of COVID-19 infected individuals. Besides lockdowns and closing of businesses, countries also prioritzed the control on international travel.

The choropleth only shows data for the top ten countries based on their incoming international arrivals. Days with bans on high-risk regions, and full border closure are also included.

Who's the most impacted?

Besides border closure, quaranting upon arrival was a huge factor in the decline of international travel. At the peak of COVID infections and even up to the end of 2021, quarantining was around 5 to 14 days long, and travelers weren't willing to spend the entirety of their vacation time in a hotel room.

Amongst the most visited countries, which ones has been the most effected by the decline in the tourism sector?

Dry Spell

At the end of 2020, global international arrivals dropped 73%.

Countries that enforced measures early on like Turkey recovered quicker in terms of tourism. Suprisingly, Mexico, who refused to close their borders all throughout the pandemic, was doing much better than countries like the US and UK. On the other hand, Thailand, one of the most dependent on tourism, is seeing a rather slow rise even after reopening majority of the country in late 2021.

    The % change displayed on the line charts are being compared the average annual international arrivals in 2019

Recovery

Global tourism has risen 4% in 2021, but still sits 72% lower than 2019 in terms of international arrivals. Asian countries are seeing a slower comeback compared to the rest of the world as they just begin to open borders and implement inital saftey procedures to travelers visiting. According to the World Travel and Tourism Council, the tourism industry should climb back to pre-pandemic numbers by 2023.
